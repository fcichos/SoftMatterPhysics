    "## Flows and Transport in Liquids\n",
    "\n",
    "### Diffusion and Brownian Motion\n",
    "\n",
    "- particles are moving in soft materials all the time→thermal fluctuations, Brownian motion\n",
    "- in addition flows, friction→hydrodynamics, mechanical properties\n",
    "- elasticity, rheology\n",
    "\n",
    "**a) Continuity**\n",
    "\n",
    "flux $\\vec{j}=ρ\\vec{n}$\n",
    "Consider a volume surrounded by a surface $S$ flow through surface:\n",
    "\n",
    "\\begin{equation}\n",
    "\\int_S\\vec{j}\\cdot\\vec{n}\\,\\mathrm dS\n",
    "\\end{equation}\n",
    "\n",
    "material in volume, e.g., particle density:\n",
    "\n",
    "\\begin{equation}\n",
    "\\int_V f \\,\\mathrm dV\n",
    "\\frac{\\mathrm d}{\\mathrm dt}\\int f \\, \\mathrm dV=\\int_V\\frac{\\mathrm df}{\\mathrm dt}\\, \\mathrm dV\n",
    "\\end{equation}\n",
    "\n",
    "conservation of matter:\n",
    "\n",
    "\\begin{equation}\n",
    "\\int_S\\vec{j}\\cdot\\vec{n}\\,\\mathrm dS=-\\int_V\\frac{\\mathrm df}{\\mathrm dt}\\,\\mathrm dV\n",
    "\\end{equation}\n",
    "\n",
    "Due to Gauss’s law\n",
    "\n",
    "\\begin{equation}\n",
    "\\int_S\\vec j \\cdot \\vec n \\,\\mathrm dS=\\int_V \\mathrm{div}\\,\\vec j \\,\\mathrm dV\n",
    "\\end{equation}\n",
    "\n",
    "and thus\n",
    "\\begin{equation}\n",
    "\\int_V \\left(\\frac{\\mathrm df}{\\mathrm dt}+\\vec\\nabla \\cdot \\vec j\\right)\\mathrm dV=0\n",
    "\\end{equation}\n",
    "\n",
    "This yields the continuity equation\n",
    "\n",
    "\\begin{equation}\n",
    "\\frac{\\mathrm df}{\\mathrm dt}+\\vec \\nabla\\cdot\\vec j = 0.\n",
    "\\end{equation}\n",
    "\n",
    "For the density $n=f$, $j ⃗=n⋅v ⃗$\n",
    "\n",
    "\\begin{equation}\n",
    "\\frac{\\partial n}{\\partial t}+\\vec \\nabla(n\\vec v)=0\n",
    "\\end{equation}\n",
    "\n",
    "for $n=\\rm const.$ it is $\\vec \\nabla⋅\\vec{V}=0 $, $\\vec \\nabla⋅\\vec j=0$\n",
    "incompressible\n",
    "\n",
    "**Diffusion** \n",
    "\n",
    "$N(x)$, $N(x+Δx)$, $0.5⋅N(x)$ to the right, $0.5⋅N(x+Δx)$ to the left net number $-1⁄2 (N(x+Δx)-N(x))$\n",
    "thus\n",
    "\n",
    "\\begin{equation}\n",
    "j=\\frac{-\\tfrac 1 2 (N(x+\\Delta x)-N(x))}{A \\,\\tau}\n",
    "\\end{equation}\n",
    "\n",
    "With the number density of particles $n(x)≡N(x)⁄Aτ$ one obtains\n",
    "\n",
    "\\begin{equation}\n",
    "j=-\\frac 1 2 \\frac{n(x+\\Delta x) \\, A \\, \\Delta x -n(x)\\,A\\,\\Delta x}{A \\,\\tau}= -\\frac 1 2 \\frac{(\\Delta x)^2}{\\tau}\\frac{n(x+\\Delta x)-n(x)}{\\Delta x}\n",
    "\\end{equation}\n",
    "\n",
    "For $Δx→0$ it is\n",
    "\n",
    "\\begin{equation}\n",
    "j=-D \\frac{\\partial n}{\\partial x}\n",
    "\\end{equation}\n",
    "\n",
    "with $D=(Δx)^2/2τ$.\n",
    "In 3d, this is\n",
    "\n",
    "\\begin{equation}\n",
    "\\vec j=-D\\vec \\nabla n\n",
    "\\end{equation}\n",
    "\n",
    "This is Fick’s first law (1855, Fick, empirical).\n",
    "flux from a gradient is due to fluctuations in the velocity, diffusion max. entropy to vanish the gradient\n",
    "\n",
    "**Combination with the continuity equation**\n",
    "\n",
    "in 1d:\n",
    "\n",
    "\\begin{equation}\n",
    "\\frac{\\mathrm dn}{\\mathrm dt}=-\\frac{\\partial j}{\\partial x}\n",
    "\\frac{\\partial j}{\\partial x}=-D\\frac{\\partial ^2n}{\\partial x^2}=-\\frac{\\partial n}{\\partial t}\n",
    "\\end{equation}\n",
    "\n",
    "Fick’s second law:\n",
    "\n",
    "\\begin{equation}\n",
    "\\frac{\\partial n}{\\partial t}=D\\nabla^2n\n",
    "\\end{equation}\n",
    "\n",
    "$n f$ could be different quantities\n",
    "\n",
    "\n",
    "| flux   | transport property | gradient  |\n",
    "| :------ | :----------:     | :----------: |\n",

    "### Smoluchowski-diffusion in an external potential\n",
    "\n",
    "\n",
    "**Smoluchowski-diffusion in an external potential**\n",
    "\n",
    "\\begin{equation}\n",
    "F=-\\frac{\\mathrm dU}{\\mathrm dx}\n",
    "\\end{equation}\n",
    "\n",
    "velocity of the particle\n",
    "\n",
    "\\begin{equation}\n",
    "v=-\\frac 1 \\xi \\frac{\\partial U}{\\partial x}\n",
    "\\end{equation}\n",
    "\n",
    "$ξ$is the friction coefficient. For a sphere $ξ=6πηR$. flux due to flow with $v$, $j_v=nv$\n",
    "\n",
    "\\begin{equation}\n",
    "j=-D\\frac{\\partial n}{\\partial x}-\\frac n \\xi \\frac{\\partial U}{\\partial x}\n",
    "\\end{equation}\n",
    "\n",
    "in steady state $j=0$ and\n",
    "\n",
    "\\begin{equation}\n",
    "n=n_0\\,\\mathrm{exp}\\left({-\\frac{U}{k_{\\mathrm B}T}}\\right)\n",
    "0=-D\\frac{\\mathrm d}{\\mathrm dx}\\left(n_0\\,\\mathrm{exp}\\left({-\\frac{U}{k_{\\mathrm B}T}}\\right)\\right)-\\frac 1 \\xi\\frac{\\mathrm d U}{\\mathrm d x}n_0\\,\\mathrm{exp}\\left({-\\frac{U}{k_{\\mathrm B}T}}\\right)+\\frac{D}{k_{\\mathrm B}T}\\frac{\\mathrm d U}{\\mathrm d x}n_0\\,\\mathrm{exp}\\left({-\\frac{U}{k_{\\mathrm B}T}}\\right)=-\\frac 1 \\xi \\frac{\\mathrm d U}{\\mathrm d x}n_0\\,\\mathrm{exp}\\left({-\\frac{U}{k_{\\mathrm B}T}}\\right)\n",
    "\\end{equation}\n",
    "\n",
    "With the diffusion coefficient $D=k_B T/ξ$ one has\n",
    "\n",
    "\\begin{equation}\n",
    "j=-\\frac 1 \\xi \\left(k_{\\mathrm B}T \\frac{\\partial n}{\\partial x}+n\\frac{\\partial U}{\\partial x}\\right)=-\\frac 1 \\xi n\\frac{\\partial}{\\partial x}(k_{\\mathrm B}T\\cdot \\ln(n)+U),\n",
    "\\end{equation}\n",
    "\n",
    "since\n",
    "\\begin{equation}\n",
    "\\frac{\\mathrm d}{\\mathrm d x}\\ln(n(x))=\\frac 1 n \\frac{\\mathrm du}{\\mathrm dx}\n",
    "\\end{equation}\n",
    "and thus\n",
    "\\begin{equation}\n",
    "\\frac{\\mathrm du}{\\mathrm dx}=n\\cdot \\ln(n).\n",
    "\\end{equation}\n",
    "\n",
    "Here, $k_B T⋅\\ln{n}+U$ is the chemical potential.\n",
    "\n",
    "\\begin{equation}\n",
    "j=-\\frac 1 \\xi n \\frac{\\partial \\mu}{\\partial x}\n",
    "\\end{equation}\n",
    "\n",
    "The chemical potential is constant in equilibrium, extend potential for ?? $\\mu=\\rm const$ .\n",
    "\n",
    "\\begin{equation}\n",
    "U=-k_{\\mathrm B}T \\ln(n(x))+\\rm const.\n",
    "\\end{equation}\n",
    "\n",
    "With Fick’s second law\n",
    "\n",
    "\\begin{equation}\n",
    "\\frac{\\partial n}{\\partial t}=-\\frac{\\partial j}{\\partial x}=\\frac 1 \\xi \\frac{\\partial}{\\partial x}\\left(k_{\\mathrm B}T\\frac{\\partial U}{\\partial x}+n\\frac{\\partial U}{\\partial x}\\right)\n",
    "\\frac{\\partial n}{\\partial t}=\\vec \\nabla\\left(D\\vec \\nabla n+\\frac{\\vec{F_{\\rm ext}}}{\\xi}n\\right)\n",
    "\\end{equation}\n",
    "\n",
    "This is the Smoluchowski equation.\n",
    "More general: Fokker—Planck equation\n",
    "\n",
    "\\begin{equation}\n",
    "\\frac{\\partial p(x,t)}{\\partial t}=-\\frac{\\partial}{\\partial x}\\left[\\frac{1}{\\xi (x,t)}p(x,t)\\right]+\\frac{\\partial^2}{\\partial x^2}\\left[D(x,t)p(x,t)\\right]\n",
    "\\end{equation}\n",
    "\n",
    "$D=k_B T/ξ$ gives a connection between thermal position fluctuations and viscous friction (fluctuation—dissipartion) $D=k_B T/6πηR$ for sphere, e.g., protein: $R\\equiv  3 \\, \\rm{nm},\\, D=100 \\,\\rm{\\mu m^2/s},\\, \\eta=0.7\\, \\rm{mPas}$.\n",
    "Solution of the diffusion equation\n",
    "\n",
    "\\begin{equation}\n",
    "\\frac{\\partial}{\\partial t}n(\\vec r, t |\\vec{r_0}, t_0)=D\\nabla^2n(\\vec r, t |\\vec{r_0}, t_0)\n",
    "\\end{equation}\n",
    "\n",
    "initial condition $n(\\vec r,t→t_0 |\\vec{r_0 },t_0 )=δ(\\vec r-\\vec{r_0 } )$\n",
    "\n",

    "\n",

    "\\end{equation}\n",
    "\n",
    "This is the Green’s function for the diffusion equation for the given boundary conditions. It propagates the initial conditions through the ??? space.\n",
